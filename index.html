The Scarlet Letter

by Nathaniel Hawthorne

In a moment, however, wisely judging that one token of her shame would but poorly serve to hide another, she took the baby on her arm, and, with a burning blush, and yet a haughty smile, and a glance that would not be abashed, looked around at her neighbors. On the breast of her gown,in the red cloth, surrounded with an elaborate embroidery and fantastic flourishes of gold thread, appeared the letter A. It was so artistically done, and with so much fertility and gorgeous luxuriance of fancy, that had all the effect of a last and fitting decoration to the apparel which she wore.
